Aeration and Dethatching

This reduces the compaction of the surface and provides oxygen and vital nutrients to the soil. With the passage of time, patches of grass gather above the layer of soil and block the passage of air into the surface. This is called thatching.

We provide specific dethatching and aeration services to reduce the thatch maintaining its required amount in the soil. Lawn fertilization and aeration is a great way to boost the health of your lawn and we help you maintain the green and healthy turf that you are looking for.

We provide aeration services in the spring and fall. Aeration involves making holes in he lawn either by pushing a rod into it by "coring" , extracting a plug of soil. By aerating your lawn you provide the following benefits to your lawn and its root system.

a) Oxygen gets to the roots and the soil allows it to "breath"
b) Organic fertilizers & nutrients get access to the root system.
c) Water is able to better soak the soil and reach the root system.
d) Helps break up thatch
e) Tight , compacted soil is loosened up allowing he root system to grow.

De-thatching:
Over time,small pieces of grass die and gather just above the soil. This is called thatch.Small amounts of thatch can be beneficial. It's organic material that is broken down by microbes in the soil. But sometimes, thatch builds up too fast for natural processes to break it down. It forms a barrier, keeping moisture and air from going where your grass needs it. ½ an inch or more of thatch can weaken your lawn.

Overseeding and Sodding

Lawnworks also provides over-seeding and sodding services throughout the year

Sodded lawns are easy to establish and provide a solid return on your landscaping investment. While the site preparation is similar for sodding or seeding an area, the maintenance required to establish healthy lawn is lower for sod.

Seed

Advantages of Seed:The initial cost of seeding a yard versus sodding is usually much less due to labour intensive costs. Certain varieties of seed can be chosen to best suit your lawns conditions. Extremely sunny or shady.

Disadvantages of Seed: A seeded yard can often take 1-3 years depending on the soil and growing conditions before it can be compared to a newly sodded yard.If a lawn becomes eroded prior to becoming fully established, it may need to be totally redone.Seeded lawns are not uniform in height and color and can appear patchy until all areas properly fill in.
